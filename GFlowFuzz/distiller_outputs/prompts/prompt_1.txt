The C Standard Library is a collection of headers that provide various functionalities for tasks such as:

1. **Error handling and reporting**: `<assert.h>`, `<errno.h>`
2. **Mathematical operations**: `<complex.h>`, `<math.h>`, `<tgmath.h>`
3. **Character and string processing**: `<ctype.h>`, `<string.h>`, `<wchar.h>`, `<wctype.h>`
4. **Input/Output operations**: `<stdio.h>`
5. **Memory management and utilities**: `<stdlib.h>`, `<stddef.h>`
6. **Integer and floating-point type limits**: `<limits.h>`, `<float.h>`, `<stdint.h>`
7. **Localization and date/time utilities**: `<locale.h>`, `<time.h>`
8. **Multithreading and synchronization**: `<threads.h>`, `<stdatomic.h>`
9. **Boolean and integer type utilities**: `<stdbool.h>`, `<stdint.h>`, `<stdckdint.h>`
10. **Miscellaneous**: `<setjmp.h>`, `<signal.h>`, `<stdalign.h>`, `<stdarg.h>`, `<stdnoreturn.h>`, `<uchar.h>`

These headers provide a wide range of functions, macros, and types that can be used to perform various tasks, from simple calculations to complex operations like multithreading and input/output processing. The C Standard Library is an essential part of the C programming language and is widely used in many applications.
